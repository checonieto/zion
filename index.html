Zion vs. Babylon.

Understanding the characteristics of a Christ-centered community based on Moses 7

"And the Lord called his people Zion, because they were of one heart and one mind, and dwelt in righteousness; and there was no poor among them." - Moses 7:18

Characteristics of Zion

From Moses 7, we learn about the society of Zion that Enoch established. These characteristics define what it means to be a Zion people:

One Heart and One Mind

Unity in purpose and love among all people.

Moses 7:18

Dwells in Righteousness

A society where people live according to God's commandments.

Moses 7:16, 18, 19

No Poor Among Them

Care for one another ensures everyone's needs are met.

Moses 7:18

The Lord Dwells With His People

God's presence is felt and acknowledged in daily life.

Moses 7:16, 69

City of Holiness

Set apart and dedicated to God's purposes.

Moses 7:19

Walk With God

Daily companionship and communion with God.

Moses 7:69

Faith Brings God's Power

Miracles follow genuine faith in Christ.

Moses 7:13

Blessed and Flourish

Prosperity in spiritual and temporal matters.

Moses 7:17

Taken Into God's Bosom

Received into divine love and protection.

Moses 7:21, 63

Prepared to Receive Christ

Ready for the Second Coming through righteous living.

Moses 7:47, 62-63
